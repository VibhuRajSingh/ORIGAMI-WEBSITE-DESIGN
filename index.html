Interesting facts about the camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Interesting facts about the cicada

  • Cicadas do not sting or bite, according to the University of Michigan.
  • When they leave their burrow, sometimes cicadas will make chimneys of mud over the exit.
  • Each species of cicada has a different song.

Interesting facts about the teddy-bear

  • The origins of Hug A Bear Day are unclear but it may be connected with the day in November 1902 when Theodore “Teddy” Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear on a hunting trip in Mississippi.
  • The incident of Roosevelt sparing the bear led to the popularity and naming of Teddy bears.
  • The Teddy Bears’ Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.

Interesting facts about the pigeon

  • THEY MIGHT BE THE FIRST DOMESTICATED BIRD.
  • THEY WON OVER CHARLES DARWIN—AND NIKOLA TESLA.
  • THEY CAN FIND THEIR WAY BACK TO THE NEST FROM 1300 MILES AWAY.

Interesting facts about the panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Interesting facts about the chameleon

  • THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.
  • ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR.
  • SMALLER CHAMELEONS HAVE FASTER TONGUES.
